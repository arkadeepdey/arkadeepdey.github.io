What this means

U-value is the rate of heat transfer through the assembly per unit area per degree of temperature difference. Lower U = better insulation. It is the reciprocal of total thermal resistance (R), which is the sum of each layer's resistance (thickness ÷ conductivity) plus the interior and exterior air-film resistances.

Material properties (density, thermal conductivity k, specific heat) and surface-film resistance values are adapted from the Bureau of Energy Efficiency, Energy Conservation Building Code (ECBC) User Guide, New Delhi.
