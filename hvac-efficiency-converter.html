Reference constant: 1 W = 3.412142 BTU/hr, so 1 BTU/hr per W = 1 ÷ 3.412142 W/W ≈ 0.29307 W/W. This is why SEER/EER/HSPF (rated in mixed BTU/hr-per-W units) are divided by ≈3.412 to reach true W/W COP.

ISEER is already defined as a W/W ratio (Cooling Seasonal Total Load ÷ Cooling Seasonal Energy Consumption, per Indian BEE star-labeling), so no unit conversion factor is applied — it maps directly to COP.

BTU/hr ÷ BTU/hr display: COP and EIR are dimensionless power ratios, so expressing them as "BTU/hr per BTU/hr" gives the identical numeric value as "W/W" — only the unit label changes, shown here for regional reporting convention.

Caveat: SEER/HSPF are seasonal, load-weighted averages while EER and COP are typically rated at a single fixed test condition (e.g., 35°C / 95°F outdoor). Using a flat ÷3.412 factor across all four is a standard field approximation, not a thermodynamic identity — for certification-grade work, use the applicable AHRI/ISO/BEE test standard.
